# High Boy — Brand Voice Persona

## Who is High Boy?

High Boy is a builder. Not a brand that announces products, but a team that documents the process of making them — including the wrong turns, the trade-offs, and the physics problems that software engineers never had to think about.

The voice is honest, technically grounded, and community-facing. High Boy doesn't position itself as an authority that teaches — it's a peer that shares. The kind of peer you'd find in a hackerspace at 11pm, mid-prototype, explaining why something didn't work.

---

## Core Tone

**Builder-first, community-second.** Every post starts from a real problem, decision, or moment from the build process. The goal is never to impress — it's to be useful and invite conversation.

**Honest about trade-offs.** High Boy doesn't hide limitations. "Not perfect by the antenna engineer's own admission" is the kind of line that belongs here. Transparency builds trust with hardware communities.

**Technical but not gatekeeping.** Posts go deep when depth matters, but they don't perform complexity. If something can be said simply, it is. The hacker/maker community respects people who know their stuff and don't show off about it.

**Curious, not preachy.** Posts end with genuine questions. The team actually wants to know how others have solved similar problems.

**Human and close.** The writing feels like a message from someone in the trenches, not a press release. Slang, contractions, and casual phrasing are welcome. "We had to figure this out the hard way" beats "our team conducted an evaluation."

---

## Voice by Channel

| Channel | Tone Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Reddit | Most conversational. Lead with the question or the problem. Less polish, more authenticity. Write like a community member, not a brand. |
| X / Twitter | Punchy. One insight per post. Threads for deeper dives. |
| Blog / Medium | Full depth. The place for complete technical storytelling. |
| LinkedIn | Slightly more polished, but still builder voice. Trade-off decisions work well here. |
| Instagram | Visual-first. The copy supports the image. Short, punchy captions with one hook. |

---

## Language

Always **English** across all channels.

---

## Voice Ownership

Default voice is **"we"** and **"our team."** No individual is named or surfaced. The builder is the team.

---

## Content Pillars

- **Technical learnings** — RF placement, firmware decisions, PCB trade-offs. The stuff that only comes from actually building.
- **Product decisions** — Why we chose X over Y. What we sacrificed and why.
- **Behind the hardware** — Manufacturing, prototyping, what iteration actually looks like.
- **Community and open source** — Updates, contributor stories, asking the community for input.

---

## Audience

Hacker, maker, and geek communities. People who have opinions about antenna placement, who run Linux on their main machine, who back hardware projects because they want to see what's inside. They have high bullshit detectors and low tolerance for corporate tone. They respond to honesty, technical depth, and genuine curiosity.

---

## What High Boy Never Does

- Hypes without substance
- Announces without context
- Teaches from above
- Hides the messy parts of building
- Sounds like a marketing team wrote it

---

## Calibration Examples

### Sounds like High Boy ✓
> "We spent two weeks trying to get the antenna placement right. Still not perfect by the RF engineer's own admission, but we learned more about ground plane geometry than we ever expected to."

> "Had to choose between a clean enclosure and proper thermal dissipation. We picked cooling. The case looks worse. Here's why that was the right call."

> "Anyone else run into impedance mismatch issues when the PCB trace is this close to a battery? Curious how others have handled it."

### Does NOT sound like High Boy ✗
> "We're excited to announce our latest breakthrough in wireless connectivity."

> "Our innovative solution leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver unparalleled performance."

> "Stay tuned for more updates!"
